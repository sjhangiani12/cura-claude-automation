# LP letter format guide

Solo GP LP letters are creative writing. They're brand. Each GP's letter should feel distinctive — not templatized. This guide is a backbone, not a constraint.

## Length norms

- **Solo GP, Fund I (small):** 1000-2000 words. LPs read end-to-end.
- **Solo GP, Fund II/III (mid):** 2000-3500 words. More portcos, more substance per portco.
- **Multi-partner emerging fund:** 3000-5000 words. Multiple voices possible.

Check the GP's prior letters to see where they land. Match.

## Default structure (override with prior-letter structure if it exists)

### 1. Opening

1-2 paragraphs. The GP's framing of the quarter — "what was true this quarter that wasn't last quarter." This is where the GP's brand voice is most visible.

Examples of opening patterns:
- **Thesis-anchored** — "This quarter reinforced our long-running bet on [thesis hook]…"
- **Macro-anchored** — "The market shifted on [trend] this quarter, and our portfolio…"
- **Personal-anchored** — "I spent this quarter focused on [activity], and what I learned…"
- **News-anchored** — "Two of our portcos hit major milestones this quarter…"

Pick the one that matches the GP's prior openings. If they don't have a consistent pattern, default to **news-anchored** — leading with concrete portfolio events is the lowest-risk opener.

### 2. Portfolio updates

This is the bulk of the letter. Per-portco entries, each 2-4 sentences (sometimes a paragraph for major news).

**Order by salience, not alphabet.** Lead with:
- Major news (raises, exits, shutdowns)
- Mark-ups / mark-downs
- Strategic shifts
- Then steady-state portcos

**Per-portco entry pattern:**

```
**[Portco Name] (we led seed in [year])**: [1-3 sentences on what happened this quarter — substantive, specific, cited]. [Optional 1 sentence on what we're watching for next quarter.]
```

Variants — adapt to the GP's voice:
- Some GPs use the company name as a header, then prose underneath
- Some integrate portco updates into flowing paragraphs
- Some use compact tables for a "metrics snapshot" + prose for narrative

**Tone for portco updates:**
- **Wins** — celebrate concretely, not generically. "Hit $1M ARR" not "had a great quarter."
- **Challenges** — name them. "Sales cycle is longer than we expected; team is iterating on pricing." Not "facing some headwinds."
- **Unknowns** — fine to acknowledge. "Still too early to call."

**What NOT to write per portco:**
- Generic enthusiasm ("incredibly excited about this team!")
- Hype claims without citation ("on track to be the leader in their category")
- Vague timelines ("major announcements coming soon")

### 3. New investments

If any closed this quarter, 1 paragraph each:

```
**[Portco Name] — [stage], [our position]**

[1-2 sentences: who they are, what they do, what traction they had at our entry.] [1-2 sentences: why this fits our thesis — connect to the named thesis hooks from prior letters or the fund deck.] [Optional 1 sentence on the founder.]
```

If multiple new investments share a thematic thread (e.g., 3 dev-tool deals), introduce that thread before listing them.

### 4. Fund operations

Concise paragraph(s) on fund-level activity. Common items:
- New LP closes (if applicable — be careful about disclosing other LPs' identities without permission)
- Capital calls completed
- Hires (analyst, EA, ops)
- AGM date / venue
- Audit / annual letter cadence reminders

If nothing meaningful happened on fund ops this quarter, omit this section. Don't pad.

### 5. Macro / thesis evolution (optional)

Only include if the GP's prior letters have this. Patterns:
- A short essay-style section on a market trend the GP is watching
- An update on how thesis sub-themes are evolving
- Commentary on competitive fund activity / deal-flow signal

If the GP doesn't do this in prior letters, don't manufacture it. AI-generated macro takes are obvious to LPs.

### 6. Asks (optional)

If the GP's prior letters have asks at the bottom:
- Customer/design-partner intros for portcos
- Hiring asks for specific roles at portcos
- LP referrals if the fund is in market for the next fund

Format: short list, specific. "Looking for: VP Eng candidates for [portco] — Series B-stage, infra background."

### 7. Closing

1-2 sentences. The GP's signoff style. Match prior letters.

Examples:
- "As always, reach out anytime."
- "More next quarter."
- "Thank you for backing us."

## What makes an LP letter sound LIKE a real GP

These are the markers LPs unconsciously check for:

1. **Specific numbers, not ranges.** "$1.2M ARR" not "low seven figures."
2. **Named people with roles.** "Maya Chen, founder of Beacon Labs," not "the team at one of our portcos."
3. **Concrete events with dates.** "Closed the lead at $3M post in February" not "raised earlier this year."
4. **Personal voice tics.** Em-dashes if the GP uses them. Lowercase opening if that's their style. The casual aside if they include them.
5. **Honest acknowledgments of challenges.** "Two portcos struggled this quarter and I'm still figuring out the right level of involvement" beats "everything is going great."
6. **Connection to prior letters.** "Building on what I wrote last quarter about X..." — implies continuity, builds the GP's narrative arc with LPs over time.

## What signals AI-generated and should be ruthlessly removed

- "It's been an exciting quarter for our portfolio."
- "We continue to be impressed by the team's execution."
- "We remain bullish on the long-term opportunity."
- "We're thrilled to have backed [X]."
- "The team is executing exceptionally well."
- Most uses of: *exciting, compelling, robust, strategic, transformative, paradigm-shifting, generational.*
- Lists of three parallel adjectives.
- Section transitions that say "Moving on..." or "Turning to..."
- A "Looking ahead" section that says nothing concrete.

When you finish drafting, scan for these and replace.

## A note on confidentiality

LP letters discuss portco metrics that founders may not want in the public domain. Default to:
- Aggregated metrics over per-deal financials
- "Strong revenue growth" only when accompanied by a concrete number — or skip the metric
- No customer names without permission (a portco saying "we closed a Fortune 500 customer" doesn't authorize naming them in the LP letter)

If the GP's prior letters reveal this judgment, copy it. If not, default to conservative.
