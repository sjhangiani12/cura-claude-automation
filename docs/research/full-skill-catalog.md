# Full skill catalog — Solo GP Workflow Enumeration

Research compiled 2026-05-04. Goes deep on the texture of solo GP / small-fund work, including small/situational/embarrassing tasks that don't appear in headline workflow articles. 186 enumerated activities across 15 categories.

This is an OVER-SPEC for Monet's roadmap. Use to prune from, not as a build list.

Per-item metadata:
- **Frequency**: daily / weekly / monthly / quarterly / per-deal / per-LP / annual / situational
- **AI verdict**: Sweet Spot (build) / Marginal (could help, easy to over-engineer) / Low-Leverage (AI gets in the way or adds little)

## Summary verdict

**Highest-leverage Cura/Monet bets** (high frequency, structured, AI excels):
- **Post-meeting interstitial pack** (#36-44) — the daily grind, biggest absolute time sink
- **Inbound triage suite** (#1-10) — volume + judgment combination
- **Diligence note synthesis + memo drafting** (#11-35)
- **DDQ / LP letter / capital call admin pack** (#56-74, #101)
- **Embarrassing-but-real relationship maintenance** (#147-159) — underserved, big differentiator
- **Decision-support memos** (#160-170)
- **LP relationship prep + recap** (#171-181)

**Avoid building:**
- Anything emotionally sensitive with founders in crisis (#45, #95, #152)
- Wire fraud verification (#123)
- Anything where GP reputation is on the line in real-time without review

**Surprising sweet spots that competitors miss:**
- Promise-tracking / "what did I say I'd do" (#44, #149, #153)
- Solo IC self-grilling — adversarial pushback you don't have a partner for (#33)
- Anti-portfolio analysis (#138)
- Side letter / MFN compliance tracking (#70-71, #105)
- Source-of-best-deals analytics (#143)
- DDQ drafting (#101) — massive time sink, highly templated, GPs hate doing it
